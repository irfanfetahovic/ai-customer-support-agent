# Wireless Earbuds Pro — Troubleshooting Guide

## Connection and Pairing Issues

### Earbuds Not Appearing in Bluetooth List
1. Place both earbuds in the charging case, close the lid, wait 10 seconds, then open the lid.
2. On the first pairing, press and hold the button on the back of the case for 5 seconds until the LED flashes white rapidly — this puts the earbuds into pairing mode.
3. On your device, go to Bluetooth settings and look for "Earbuds Pro" in the available devices list.
4. If they still do not appear, try on a different device to rule out a phone Bluetooth issue.

### Earbuds Previously Paired But No Longer Connecting
- Earbuds remember up to 8 paired devices. If the list is full, new devices replace the oldest entry.
- On your phone, forget "Earbuds Pro" in Bluetooth settings, then re-pair from scratch.
- To clear all pairings from the earbuds: with earbuds removed from the case, hold both touch surfaces simultaneously for 10 seconds until you hear "Pairing list cleared."

### Only One Earbud Connecting
1. Place both earbuds in the case for 15 seconds.
2. Remove both earbuds at the same time — they sync with each other before connecting to the phone.
3. If only one earbud ever charges (check LED indicator when placing in case), the contact pins may need cleaning.

### Multipoint — Switching Between Devices Not Working
- Multipoint pairing is enabled by default. The earbuds connect to the two most recently used devices.
- To switch to a different device, pause playback on the current device, then play audio on the target device.
- Multipoint can be toggled in the SoundSync app: Devices → Earbuds Pro → Multipoint.

## Audio Quality Issues

### No Audio From One Earbud
If one earbud produces no sound while the other works normally:

1. **Charge** — Place both earbuds in the case for 15 minutes; a dead earbud may appear connected but produce no audio.
2. **Clean mesh** — Gently brush the grille with a dry soft brush; do not use liquid or sharp objects.
3. **Re-seat** — Return both to case, close lid 15 s, then remove both together so they re-sync.
4. **Re-pair** — Forget "Earbuds Pro" in Bluetooth settings and pair fresh to clear any corrupted profile.
5. **Factory reset** — Follow the Factory Reset procedure below; this clears all settings and is the most reliable fix.
6. **Escalate** — No audio after reset = likely hardware defect; contact support — covered under warranty if within the warranty period.

### Muffled or Low-Volume Sound
1. Check ear-tip fit — the correct ear-tip creates a light seal in the ear canal. Try a larger size.
2. Clean the mesh grille on the earbuds with a dry, soft-bristled brush. Do not use water or liquid cleaners.
3. Check the EQ settings in the SoundSync app — a heavy bass boost can make the sound seem muffled.
4. Check the volume on both the phone and the earbuds are at reasonable levels (not both at maximum).

### ANC Not Blocking Enough Noise
- ANC performance depends heavily on the ear-tip seal. Try a larger ear-tip size.
- ANC is most effective for low-frequency steady sounds (airplane engine hum, AC noise). It is less effective at high-frequency or sudden sounds (voices, alarms).
- Ensure ANC is set to "Maximum" in the SoundSync app (it defaults to "Adaptive").
- In very windy conditions, ANC may reduce itself automatically to prevent wind-noise amplification.

### Audio Cutting Out During Playback
1. Check for interference sources near you (Wi-Fi routers, other Bluetooth devices, microwave ovens).
2. Ensure the phone is within 10 metres with no thick walls between you and the phone.
3. On Android, disable Bluetooth Audio Codec "Best effort" mode and set it to aptX or AAC manually in Developer Options.
4. Reset the earbuds (see Factory Reset below) and re-pair.

## Microphone and Call Quality

### Callers Cannot Hear You Clearly
1. Ensure the earbuds are not covered by hair, clothing, or anything blocking the microphone mesh.
2. Gently clean the microphone meshes with a dry brush — do not insert any object into the mesh holes.
3. In the SoundSync app, ensure "Call wind noise reduction" is enabled.
4. Test with the earbuds' built-in mic by making a call without any app-level processing.

## Battery and Charging

### Earbuds Not Charging in Case
1. Check that the earbuds are seated properly in the case — the earbud stem should click into the indent.
2. Clean the charging contacts on both the earbuds and inside the case with a dry cotton swab.
3. Charge the case itself via USB-C — the case LED turns red when the case battery is critically low.
4. Try wireless Qi charging to isolate whether the USB-C port on the case is the issue.

### Battery Life Significantly Below Spec
- Battery degrades naturally over time. After 300–500 full charge cycles, capacity may be reduced by 10–20%.
- ANC being enabled at all times is the biggest drain; disabling ANC extends earbud battery from ~5.5 hours to ~8 hours.
- Ensure earbuds are stored in the case when not in use so they enter low-power sleep mode.

## Factory Reset
**Warning:** This clears all paired device history and custom settings from the earbuds. SoundSync app preferences are stored in the app and will re-apply after re-pairing.

Steps:
1. Remove earbuds from the case.
2. Touch and hold both earbud surfaces simultaneously for 10 seconds.
3. You will hear a chime and see the case LED flash three times.
4. Re-pair with your device.

## When to Contact Support
Contact support if the issue persists, or if:
- An earbud does not charge at all after cleaning contacts
- An earbud has no audio output from one side
- Touch controls are completely unresponsive after a reset
- The issue is likely a manufacturing defect covered under warranty
