# Escalation Playbook

## How Customers Can Request Escalation

If your issue has not been resolved after two attempts, or you would like to speak with a manager or senior agent, you can request escalation at any time.

**Steps to escalate your issue:**
1. Tell the support agent directly: *"I would like to escalate this issue to a manager or specialist."*
2. The agent will open a priority escalation ticket on your behalf and assign it to the appropriate specialist team.
3. You will receive a confirmation with a **ticket reference number** and an expected response time.
4. A senior agent or specialist will follow up via your preferred contact method (email or phone).

**Expected response times after escalation:**
| Priority | Situation | Response Time |
|---|---|---|
| Urgent | Safety, fraud, account takeover | Within 2 hours |
| High | Unresolved after 2+ attempts, legal concern | Same business day |
| Normal | Complex billing or product issue | Next business day |

**What to have ready when escalating:**
- Your account ID or the email address on your account
- Your order ID (if applicable)
- A brief summary of the issue and the steps already tried
- Your preferred callback time and contact method

---

## When to Escalate

Escalate to a human agent or specialist team when **any** of the following are true:
- The customer has asked to speak to a human agent or manager
- The issue has not been resolved after two full resolution attempts
- The agent is unable to find the customer's account or order in the CRM
- The customer is threatening legal action, a regulatory complaint, or social media escalation
- Sensitive situations: suspected fraud, data breach concerns, safety issues
- The required action (e.g., large refund, account deletion, chargeback investigation) requires human authorisation

---

## Escalation Tiers

| Tier | Team | Handles |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | Senior Support Agent | Complex product issues, large refunds ($100–$500), repeated contacts |
| Tier 2 | Finance Team | Billing disputes over $500, chargebacks, duplicate charge investigations |
| Tier 2 | Account Security Team | Suspected account takeover, MFA reset, identity verification failures |
| Tier 3 | Legal / Compliance | Legal threats, regulatory complaints (GDPR, CCPA), subpoenas |

---

## How to Escalate

### Internal Escalation (handoff within the same session)
1. Inform the customer: *"I'm going to connect you with a specialist who can help resolve this more quickly."*
2. Do **not** put the customer on hold for more than 2 minutes without checking back.
3. Brief the receiving agent with: customer name, account ID, issue summary, steps already taken.

### Async Escalation (ticket handoff)
1. Create a detailed escalation ticket in the CRM including:
   - Customer account ID and contact details
   - Order ID(s) involved
   - Full summary of the issue and all steps already taken
   - Recommended next action
   - Customer's preferred contact method and availability
2. Set ticket priority: **Urgent** (response within 2 hours), **High** (same business day), **Normal** (next business day).
3. Inform the customer: *"I've escalated this to our specialist team. You'll receive a follow-up by [time/date] via [email/phone]."*

---

## Communication Guidelines During Escalation

- Never tell a customer that their issue is "not possible" to resolve without checking with the appropriate team.
- Never promise a specific outcome (e.g., "you will definitely get a refund") before it has been approved.
- Always give a concrete callback or response time — vague commitments damage trust.
- Use empathy: *"I completely understand your frustration, and I want to make sure this gets resolved properly for you."*

---

## Post-Escalation
- Log the escalation reason and outcome in the CRM case.
- If the escalation was due to a product or policy gap, note it so the feedback can be reviewed by the product/policy team.
- Close the original ticket only after confirmation from the specialist team that the issue has been resolved.
